FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Is FirePlayer free to download?

Yes. FirePlayer is a free download from the App Store and runs on iPhone, iPad and Mac.

What is FirePlayer?

FirePlayer is a music player built around your MediaFire account. It streams and downloads your songs so you can enjoy your entire collection without filling up your device storage. You get offline playback, custom playlists and a built-in equalizer, and your tracks are neatly organized by artist, album, genre and composer. It also supports gapless playback, crossfade, and casting to Chromecast or AirPlay.

How does FirePlayer work?

Once you link your MediaFire account, FirePlayer lets you stream any song instantly or download it for offline listening. It handles a wide range of audio formats, lets you build and manage playlists, fine-tune sound with the equalizer, cast to Chromecast or AirPlay, and even import local files stored on your device.

Is FirePlayer safe to use?

Yes. FirePlayer relies on secure SDKs and encrypted connections, and it never stores your MediaFire username or password. Sign-in happens entirely on MediaFire's own official pages, and the app keeps only a secure authorization token on your device. You can revoke that token anytime from your MediaFire account settings in any web browser. Any music you download lives inside the app's own Documents folder and can be removed with a file manager. Disconnecting your account or uninstalling FirePlayer clears the stored tokens and downloaded files.

Which audio formats does FirePlayer support?

FirePlayer plays MP3, AAC, ALAC, FLAC, WAV, AIFF and M4R with no conversion needed.

How do I create a playlist in FirePlayer?
  1. Go to the Playlists tab.
  2. Tap the "+" button, or tap "..." and choose New Playlist.
  3. Enter a name for your playlist.
  4. Tap Save.
  5. Select the tracks you want to include.
  6. Confirm your selection to finish.
How do I add songs to an existing playlist?
  1. Open the playlist you want to update.
  2. Tap the "..." button.
  3. Choose Add Songs.
  4. Pick the tracks you'd like to add.
  5. Tap Done.
How do I change the order of songs in a playlist?
  1. Open the Playlists tab and select your playlist.
  2. Tap "..." and choose Edit Playlist.
  3. Drag tracks to reorder them, or use the sort options.
How do I download music for offline playback?
  1. Open the Network tab and connect your MediaFire account.
  2. Browse into the folder that holds your music.
  3. Tap "..." and choose Select.
  4. Pick your tracks and tap Download.
How do I play files directly from MediaFire?
  1. Open the Network tab and connect MediaFire.
  2. Browse into the folder containing your songs.
  3. Tap a file to add it to the queue and start playing.
How do I manage my files on MediaFire?

Open the Network tab and connect your account. Tap "..." to select files, then rename, move, delete or download them directly from within FirePlayer.

How do I use the equalizer?
  1. Open the Audio Player.
  2. Tap the Equalizer icon.
  3. Turn the equalizer on.
  4. Pick a preset or adjust the custom sliders to taste.
How do I stream music to Chromecast or AirPlay?
  1. Open the Audio Player.
  2. Tap the Audio Speaker icon.
  3. Select your Chromecast or AirPlay device from the list.
How do I turn on the Sleep Timer?
  1. Open Settings.
  2. Go to Audio Player.
  3. Tap Sleep Timer and choose your interval.
How do I set up gapless playback?

Open Settings, go to Audio Player, and switch on Gapless Playback. Availability can vary depending on your device and iOS version.

How can I resume a playlist where I left off?

In Settings, open Audio Player and enable Save Audio Player State. After that, you can pick up right where you stopped by choosing Continue Playback.

How do I transfer music from my computer?

You have two options: use Wi-Fi Drive while your computer and device are on the same Wi-Fi network, or use iTunes/Finder File Sharing over a cable.

How do I use Wi-Fi Drive to transfer files?
  1. Open the Network tab and choose Computer.
  2. Tap Connect using Wi-Fi and turn it on.
  3. Open the shown URL in a browser on your desktop.
  4. Drag and drop your files into the browser window.
  5. Tap Stop Wi-Fi Drive when you're finished.
How do I move my existing music library to MediaFire?
  1. Sign up for a MediaFire account.
  2. Install the MediaFire desktop app on your computer.
  3. Upload your music files to MediaFire.
  4. Sign in to that same account in FirePlayer.
How can I delete my music library?

Open Settings, go to Music Library and tap Delete Music Library. This only clears the local database on your device. Your files on MediaFire and any songs you've already downloaded stay untouched.
